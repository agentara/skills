# Selection Criteria
- **Causality**: Strong cause-effect chain vs. episodic events?
- **Character Arc**: Internal transformation needed vs. consistent character?
- **Goal Clarity**: Clear external objective vs. open-ended exploration?
- **Twist Dependency**: Relies on surprise/reversal vs. linear progression?
- **Emotional Tone**: Action, comedy, warmth, contemplation, suspense?
- **Completeness**: Full story arc vs. single moment vs. open ending?

# Available Methodologies (5 Core Types)

## 1. story_spine
**When to use**: Linear causality with "Because of that" progression, clear external goal, event-driven
**Structure**: once_upon_a_time → every_day (optional) → until_one_day → because_of_that_1 → because_of_that_2 → because_of_that_3 (optional) → until_finally → ever_since_then (optional)
**Best for**: Quest narratives, adventure with goal, comedic/warm partnerships
**Key identifiers**: Search/find/looking for, going to destination, rescue mission, stated objective
**Suitable examples**: Scavenger finds robot → protects her → journey to oasis; Knight rescues princess → overcomes obstacles → returns home
**NOT suitable**: Single moments without progression, twist-dependent stories, contemplative scenes without goal

## 2. kishotenketsu
**When to use**: Non-conflict structure, emotional twist/revelation, harmony through unexpected connection
**Structure**: ki_introduction → sho_development → ten_twist → ketsu_reconciliation
**Best for**: Contemplative, poetic, nostalgic, ironic reversal, Eastern aesthetics
**Key identifiers**: Discovery of emotional object, revelation about past, memory triggered, turns out to be, no antagonist
**Suitable examples**: Old man finds camera → discovers own past photo → reconciles with memory; Strangers discover connection
**NOT suitable**: Action/combat, linear quests with goals, conflict-resolution, defeating opponents

## 3. the_moment_method
**When to use**: NO complete story needed, capture ONE peak experience, audience fills the rest
**Structure**: context_setup → peak_moment → emotional_resolution
**Best for**: Emotional climax, visual spectacle, ceremonial moments
**Key identifiers**: No cause-effect chain, no journey, no conflict resolution - ONLY a singular significant moment
**Suitable examples**: Wedding vow exchange, athlete's winning moment, musician's final note, final goodbye
**NOT suitable**: Any story with then/next progression, battles with outcomes, quests/searches, problem-solving sequences

## 4. beat_sheet
**When to use**: Combat/confrontation, dramatic tension with ups and downs, action sequences
**Structure**: opening_image → catalyst → midpoint → all_is_lost → climax → closing_image
**Best for**: Battle scenes, fights, overcoming dangerous obstacles, dramatic conflicts
**Key identifiers**: Fight/battle/combat/duel, versus/against, physical conflict, defeat/victory, confrontation
**Suitable examples**: Knight vs mage battle, escaping from monster, survival challenge, defeating guardian
**NOT suitable**: Peaceful discoveries, non-conflict narratives, simple linear progressions without tension

## 5. hook_story_cta
**When to use**: Series first episode, incomplete resolution acceptable, sequel setup
**Structure**: hook → story → cta_or_cliffhanger
**Best for**: Serial content, mystery setups, to be continued narratives
**Key identifiers**: Open ending implied, first encounter, mystery introduced, unresolved plot threads
**Suitable examples**: Detective finds first clue, hero meets mentor, villain identity hinted
**NOT suitable**: Self-contained complete stories, resolved conflicts, achieved goals

# Critical Decision Rules

**If story contains combat/fight/battle/duel** → Default to `beat_sheet`
**If story has search/find + clear goal** → Default to `story_spine`
**If story is discovery + emotional revelation without conflict** → Default to `kishotenketsu`
**If story is ONE ceremony/moment with no progression** → Default to `the_moment_method`
**If story explicitly incomplete or first episode setup** → Default to `hook_story_cta`
